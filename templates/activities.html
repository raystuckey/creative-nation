  • Motto

    Students will learn to observe closely and then to empathize with peers so that they can decide what values are important to their new nation.

    Lesson 1

  • Capital City

    After developing core values, students will build upon these values to create a 2D map and 3D model of their ‘nation’ to practice the skills of dimensional thinking and modeling. The idea of the nation will begin to take on a physical format.

    Lesson 2

  • Myth

    Nearly all cultures have a story that has been passed down through generations. These stories drive their culture, as well as share a very important aspect of their history. In this challenge students will develop a monomyth while developing the thinking skills of recognizing and forming patterns

    Lesson 3

  • Sport

    Another component to a culture’s social aspect is sports. In this challenge students will design a new sporting event (game) that allows students to play and start to understand how their past experiences of play leads to the act of creating a game. The type of physical game (sport) they create will allow them to use body thinking to engage in the sport and understand the design of how sports work and how others can participate.

    Lesson 4

  • Food

    What is another national pastime? Eating. The focus of this lesson is to help work on students’ skills of analogizing. In this instance their goal is to create a concrete object (a dish) that represents many disparate elements of their culture.

    Lesson 5

  • Flag

    Now that multiple aspects of culture and thinking have been developed, a representation of these ideas come to fruition in the making of a national flag. While practicing abstraction thinking skills students will determine the most important aspects of their culture to be represented in their creation.

    Lesson 6

  • Daily Life

    With multiple components of culture now developed for the new nation, it’s time to see what a day in the life of an individual may looks like. Students will be culminating the variety of transferable skills they have used up to this point in the creation nation process. While relating their own experiences they will be transforming and synthesizing their ideas of how a day might take shape for an individual in their created community.

    Lesson 7

  • Celebration

    Celebration of learning and opportunity for students to reflect on the process of creating a nation and consider application of creative thinking skills.

    Lesson 8
